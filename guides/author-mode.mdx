Author mode gives you complete creative control over your story's narrative while StoryFox handles the illustrations. Perfect for writers who want to craft their own tales or adapt existing stories with AI-generated artwork.

## What is author mode?

Author mode lets you:
- Write your own story title and page text
- Provide optional character descriptions for visual consistency
- Generate professional illustrations that match your narrative
- Create custom stories with your unique voice and style

Unlike Quick and Guided modes where AI writes the story, Author mode treats you as the storyteller and AI as your illustrator.

## When to choose author mode

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="You have a specific story to tell" icon="book">
    When you've already written or planned your story text and just need illustrations
  </Card>
  <Card title="You're adapting an existing tale" icon="sparkles">
    Perfect for retelling classic stories, family tales, or creating illustrated versions of your work
  </Card>
  <Card title="You want complete narrative control" icon="pen">
    When AI-generated text doesn't match your vision or writing style
  </Card>
  <Card title="You're teaching creative writing" icon="graduationcap">
    Great for educators helping students illustrate their original stories
  </Card>

## How author mode differs from other modes

| Feature | Quick Mode | Guided Mode | Author Mode |
|---------|------------|-------------|-------------|
| Story text | AI-generated | AI-generated (enriched) | You write it |
| Character descriptions | AI-generated | AI-guided | You write it (optional) |
| Image prompts | AI-generated | AI-generated | AI-generated from your text |
| Creative control | Low | Medium | High |
| Time to create | Fastest | Medium | Depends on your writing |
| Best for | Quick ideas | Detailed concepts | Custom narratives |

<Info>
  All three modes use the same AI illustration pipeline, so visual quality is identical. The difference is who writes the story.
</Info>

## Creating a story in author mode

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Author mode">
    On the creation screen, click the **Author** chip in the Creation Mode toggle. The interface switches to a page-by-page editor.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write your story title">
    Enter a title in the **Story Title** field. This appears on your book's cover.
    
    ```
    The Adventures of Luna the Fox
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add character descriptions (optional)">
    In the **Character Descriptions** field, describe your main characters to help the AI maintain visual consistency:
    
    ```
    Luna - small orange fox, green scarf, curious eyes
    ```
    
    This step is optional but strongly recommended for better illustrations.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write your story pages">
    Fill in each page with your story text. Use the **+** and **-** buttons to add or remove pages.
    
    Each page should contain 2-4 sentences that advance your narrative.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure illustration settings">
    Click the **wand and stars** button to set:
    - **Illustration Style**: Choose Illustration, Animation, or Sketch
    - (Page count is determined by how many pages you write)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate illustrations">
    Click **Illustrate Your Story** when you're ready. StoryFox will:
    
    1. Analyze your story text
    2. Generate image prompts for each page
    3. Validate character descriptions
    4. Create illustrations for the cover and all pages
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Writing your story text

### Story structure tips

<Tip>
  **Keep it focused**: Children's books work best with a single story arc. Introduce your character, present a challenge, and resolve it by the end.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Show, don't tell**: Include visual details that the AI can illustrate. Instead of "Luna was brave," try "Luna stepped forward, paws steady despite the dark forest ahead."
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Be consistent**: If you mention a red balloon on page 2, reference the same balloon later rather than introducing a new blue one.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Match your audience**: For kids ages 3-8, use simple sentences and concrete imagery. For older readers, you can use richer vocabulary.
</Tip>

### Page length guidelines

Each page should have **2-4 sentences**. This creates:
- Enough context for meaningful illustrations
- Readable text that doesn't overwhelm young readers
- Proper pacing for a children's book

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Too short">
    ```
    Luna went outside.
    ```
    
    Not enough context for a compelling illustration or narrative progression.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Just right">
    ```
    Luna stepped out into the meadow, her green scarf 
    fluttering in the breeze. Butterflies danced around 
    the wildflowers, and she felt her heart lift with joy.
    ```
    
    Clear scene, visual details, emotional context — perfect for illustration.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Too long">
    ```
    Luna stepped outside and saw the meadow was full of 
    flowers and butterflies were everywhere and the sun 
    was shining and she thought about her friend Oliver 
    who she hadn't seen in days and wondered if he would 
    come visit and maybe they could play together like 
    they used to when they were younger...
    ```
    
    Too much information makes it hard to create a focused illustration.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Providing character descriptions

Character descriptions help the AI maintain visual consistency across all illustrations. This is especially important for multi-page stories.

### What to include

<Steps>
  <Step title="Character name">
    Start with the character's name for clarity:
    
    ```
    Luna - ...
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Species and size">
    Specify what kind of animal or creature:
    
    ```
    Luna - small orange fox
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Distinctive features">
    Add visual traits that make them recognizable:
    
    ```
    Luna - small orange fox, green scarf, curious eyes
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Multiple characters (if needed)">
    Separate multiple characters with line breaks:
    
    ```
    Luna - small orange fox, green scarf, curious eyes
    Oliver - gray owl, round glasses, wise expression
    ```
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Character description examples

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Good examples">
    ```
    Spark - tiny red dragon, golden wings, friendly smile
    
    Captain Whiskers - tabby cat, blue sailor hat, 
    striped vest, adventurous spirit
    
    Rosie - pink elephant, purple ribbon in hair, 
    gentle eyes, loves flowers
    ```
    
    Each description is clear, visual, and specific enough for consistent illustration.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Avoid these">
    ```
    A dog
    ```
    Too vague — what breed, color, or features?
    
    ```
    Brave and kind hero who loves helping others
    ```
    Personality traits don't help with visuals. Include physical appearance.
    
    ```
    Small creature with various colors and different 
    accessories that change based on the scene
    ```
    Inconsistency makes character recognition impossible.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Info>
  Character descriptions are optional, but stories with clear character descriptions produce more visually consistent results.
</Info>

## How AI generates image prompts from your text

When you click **Illustrate Your Story**, StoryFox analyzes your text using Apple's Foundation Models:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Scene analysis">
    The AI reads each page and identifies:
    - Key visual elements (characters, objects, settings)
    - Actions and movements
    - Mood and atmosphere
  </Step>

  <Step title="Prompt generation">
    The AI creates a detailed illustration prompt that:
    - Describes the scene composition
    - Incorporates your character descriptions
    - Maintains children's book illustration style
    - Ensures no text appears in images
  </Step>

  <Step title="Character consistency">
    StoryFox enriches prompts to guarantee your characters look the same across all pages using the same pipeline as Quick and Guided modes.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Illustration generation">
    Each enriched prompt generates a professional illustration matching your chosen style.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  If Foundation Models is unavailable, StoryFox uses a heuristic fallback that extracts the first sentence from each page as the illustration basis.
</Info>

## Page requirements

Author mode requires **at least 4 pages** with text to generate illustrations. You can create up to 16 pages.

### Managing pages

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Add a page" icon="plus.circle">
    Click the **+** button next to the page counter. You can have up to 16 pages total.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Remove a page" icon="minus.circle">
    Click the **-** button to remove the last page. Minimum 1 page, but you need 4+ filled pages to generate.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Info>
  Empty pages are automatically filtered out during generation. Only pages with actual text content will be illustrated.
</Info>

### Why 4+ pages?

Children's books need enough pages to:
- Establish a clear beginning, middle, and end
- Create meaningful narrative progression
- Showcase character development
- Provide visual variety in illustrations

Stories shorter than 4 pages often feel incomplete or lack proper story structure.

## Tips for writing good story text

<Tip>
  **Include visual scene details**: The AI illustrates what you describe. Mention settings, objects, weather, time of day, and character positions.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Use active language**: "Luna climbed the tall oak tree" creates better illustrations than "Luna was in a tree."
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Vary your settings**: Change locations or scenes across pages to keep illustrations visually interesting.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Maintain a consistent tone**: If you start with whimsical language, keep that tone throughout. Sudden shifts confuse the visual interpretation.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Read it aloud**: If it sounds good when spoken, it's probably good for a children's book. Test pacing and flow by reading to a child or friend.
</Tip>

## Tips for character descriptions

<Tip>
  **Focus on permanent features**: Describe traits that should appear in every illustration (fur color, size, signature accessory).
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Avoid scene-specific details**: Don't say "wearing a raincoat" unless they wear it throughout the entire story. These belong in page text, not character descriptions.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Be concise**: 10-15 words per character is ideal. Long descriptions don't improve consistency.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Use species consistently**: If you call them a "fox" in the description, use "fox" in your page text too (not "creature" or "animal").
</Tip>

## Generating illustrations

Once you've written your story:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review your content">
    Make sure:
    - Title is filled in
    - At least 4 pages have text
    - Character descriptions are provided (recommended)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose your illustration style">
    Open Book Setup and select:
    - **Illustration**: Classic painted storybook style
    - **Animation**: Modern, Pixar-inspired 3D look
    - **Sketch**: Hand-drawn, artistic appearance
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Illustrate Your Story">
    The button activates when you have a title and at least one page with text.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Monitor progress">
    StoryFox shows progress as it:
    - Generates image prompts from your text
    - Validates character descriptions
    - Creates illustrations (you'll see the count increase)
  </Step>
</Steps>

The illustration phase takes 2-5 minutes depending on page count. Your story text remains exactly as you wrote it — only the images are generated.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Generate button is disabled">
    The **Illustrate Your Story** button requires:
    - A non-empty story title
    - At least one page with text (though 4+ is recommended)
    
    Check that you've filled these fields.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Safety filter blocked my story">
    If your text contains phrases that trigger content safety rules, try:
    - Using gentler, child-friendly language
    - Avoiding violent or scary descriptions
    - Rephrasing mature themes into age-appropriate metaphors
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Characters look different across pages">
    If character appearance varies:
    - Make sure you filled the **Character Descriptions** field
    - Be more specific about distinctive features (colors, accessories, species)
    - Ensure descriptions don't contradict page text
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Illustrations don't match my page text">
    The AI interprets visual scenes from your writing. To improve accuracy:
    - Include more specific visual details in your text
    - Avoid abstract concepts — focus on concrete, visible elements
    - Use shorter, clearer sentences with one main visual idea each
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I want to change the story after generation">
    Author mode doesn't save your working draft — it only saves completed books. To make changes:
    - Cancel generation if it's still running
    - Edit your text in the Author mode editor
    - Generate again with the updated text
    
    Once a book is saved, you can regenerate individual pages from the reader view.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## When to choose author mode vs other modes

### Choose Author mode when:

- You've already written your story
- You want to control the exact wording and pacing
- You're adapting a specific tale or personal story
- You're working with students on their creative writing
- AI-generated text doesn't match your vision

### Choose Quick mode when:

- You have a concept but want AI to write the story
- You need a complete book quickly
- You're exploring ideas without a specific narrative in mind
- You trust AI's storytelling abilities

### Choose Guided mode when:

- You have a concept and want to collaboratively develop it
- You need help fleshing out characters, plot, and themes
- You want more control than Quick but less work than Author
- You enjoy the Q&A creative process

<Tip>
  You can always try multiple modes with the same core idea. Start with Guided to develop your concept, then use Author mode to refine the exact wording.
</Tip>
