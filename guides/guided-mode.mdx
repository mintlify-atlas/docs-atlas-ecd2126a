Guided mode helps you develop your story concept through an interactive Q&A session with the AI. It's perfect when you have a basic idea but want help fleshing out the details.

## How guided mode works

Instead of generating a story immediately, guided mode asks you questions about:
- Characters and setting (Round 1)
- Plot and conflict (Round 2)
- Tone and resolution (Round 3)

Your answers are compiled into an enriched concept that produces more detailed, personalized stories.

## Starting a guided story

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Guided mode">
    On the creation screen, click the **Guided** chip in the Creation Mode toggle. The button changes to "Explore Your Story."
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter your basic concept">
    Type a simple story idea in the text field:
    
    > "A shy turtle who wants to make friends"
    
    You don't need to include every detail — the AI will ask about specifics.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Explore Your Story">
    This starts the Q&A flow. The AI generates the first round of questions based on your concept.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Answering questions

Each question offers **three suggested answers (A, B, C)** plus a **custom answer field (D)**.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/qa-question-card.png" alt="Question card with multiple choice options" />
</Frame>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Read the question">
    Questions are tailored to your concept and the current round. For example:
    
    - Round 1: "What kind of turtle is our hero?"
    - Round 2: "What challenge does the turtle face?"
    - Round 3: "How should the story make readers feel?"
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose your answer">
    Select one of the suggested answers by clicking it, or type your own custom answer in option D.
    
    Selected options highlight in coral with a filled badge.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Answer at least one question">
    You must answer at least one question per round to proceed. Answering all questions gives the AI more context, but isn't required.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Proceed to the next round">
    Click **Keep Going** to move to the next round, or **Generate Now** if you're ready to create the story.
    
    The final round shows a **Generate Story** button instead.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding the rounds

### Round 1: Hero & World

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="For Kids audience">
    Focuses on:
    - Character species and appearance
    - Where the story takes place
    - What makes your hero special
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="For Everyone audience">
    Focuses on:
    - Character background and motivation
    - Setting details and atmosphere
    - Character relationships
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Round 2: Adventure & Problem

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="For Kids audience">
    Focuses on:
    - What adventure happens
    - What problem or challenge appears
    - How it affects the hero
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="For Everyone audience">
    Focuses on:
    - Central conflict or tension
    - Stakes and consequences
    - Plot structure
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Round 3: Ending & Feelings

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="For Kids audience">
    Focuses on:
    - How the story ends
    - What feelings you want to create
    - What the hero learns
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="For Everyone audience">
    Focuses on:
    - Tone and emotional arc
    - Resolution approach
    - Theme and moral
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Navigation controls

While answering questions, you have several options:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Cancel" icon="xmark">
    Exits guided mode and returns to Quick mode. Your progress is not saved.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Generate Now" icon="bolt">
    Skips remaining rounds and creates the story using your answers so far.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Keep Going / Generate Story" icon="arrow.right">
    Proceeds to the next round or generates the final story.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Tips for guided mode

<Tip>
  **Use suggestions as inspiration**: The AI's suggested answers often spark better ideas than you'd think of alone. Don't feel obligated to write custom answers every time.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Be consistent**: If you say your character is shy in Round 1, consider how that affects the conflict in Round 2 and resolution in Round 3.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Don't overthink it**: Guided mode is designed to be fun and collaborative. There are no wrong answers.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Adjust audience mode first**: Set your audience (For Kids vs For Everyone) in Settings before starting. This changes the questions' focus and vocabulary.
</Tip>

## What happens after Q&A?

Once you complete the final round:

1. **Concept compilation**: Your original idea and all answers are compiled into a rich, detailed concept
2. **Automatic generation**: The story generates immediately using the enriched concept
3. **Enhanced length limit**: Guided concepts can be up to 1500 characters (vs 220 for Quick mode)

The enriched concept produces stories with:
- More detailed character personalities
- Better plot structure and pacing
- Stronger thematic consistency
- Visual descriptions that match your answers

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Questions don't match my concept">
    This can happen if your initial concept was very vague. Try:
    - Being more specific in your opening concept
    - Using custom answers (option D) to steer back on track
    - Clicking Cancel and starting over with a clearer idea
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The AI keeps asking more rounds than I want">
    The AI decides when it has enough information (up to 4 rounds max). To finish early:
    - Click **Generate Now** instead of Keep Going
    - Answer fewer questions per round
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Generation failed after Q&A">
    The error handling is the same as Quick mode:
    - Check for content safety issues
    - Verify your generation mode (Local/Cloud) is configured
    - Try editing your answers to be more child-friendly
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Comparing guided vs quick mode

| Feature | Quick Mode | Guided Mode |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Speed | Fastest | Slower (adds Q&A time) |
| Control | Basic | High |
| Detail | Good | Excellent |
| Best for | Simple concepts, speed | Complex ideas, personalization |
| Concept length | 220 characters | 1500 characters |
