StoryFox makes it easy to generate beautifully illustrated children's books using AI. This guide walks you through creating your first story from start to finish.

## Before you begin

Make sure you have:
- StoryFox installed on your Mac
- A story idea or concept in mind
- Either Apple Intelligence enabled OR a Hugging Face account for cloud generation

## Creating a story in Quick mode

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open StoryFox">
    Launch the app from your Applications folder or Dock.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter your story concept">
    In the main text field, describe your story idea. For example:
    
    > "A brave little fox who learns to share with forest friends"
    
    The more specific you are, the better your story will be. Include:
    - Main character and their traits
    - Setting or world
    - What happens or what they learn
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure book settings">
    Click the **wand and stars** button to open Book Setup:
    
    - **Page Count**: Choose 4-16 pages (even numbers only)
    - **Illustration Style**: Pick from Illustration, Animation, or Sketch
    
    These settings determine your book's length and visual style.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose generation mode">
    Select between Local or Cloud generation:
    
    - **Local**: Uses Apple Intelligence on your Mac (requires Apple silicon)
    - **Cloud**: Uses Hugging Face models (requires login in Settings)
    
    The mode picker appears next to the Book Setup button.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate your story">
    Click the **Squeeze a Story** button. StoryFox will:
    
    1. Generate your story text (title, pages, character descriptions)
    2. Validate and enrich character descriptions
    3. Create illustrations for the cover and every page
    
    This process takes 2-5 minutes depending on page count and generation mode.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review your book">
    Once generation completes, your book opens automatically in the reader view. Use the navigation arrows or swipe to browse pages.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips for better results

<Tip>
  **Be specific about your character**: Instead of "a dog," say "a golden retriever puppy with floppy ears." This helps maintain visual consistency across pages.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Keep concepts simple**: StoryFox works best with single-story arcs appropriate for ages 3-8. Complex plots or multiple storylines may not fit well.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Use the suggestion carousel**: If you're stuck for ideas, wait for the typewriter animation to show suggested story concepts you can use.
</Tip>

## What happens during generation?

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Text generation phase">
    StoryFox creates:
    - A captivating title
    - Character descriptions with species, colors, and traits
    - Story text for each page (2-4 sentences per page)
    - Image prompts describing the visual scene for each page
    
    The AI ensures every page advances the narrative and maintains a consistent tone.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Character validation (on-device only)">
    When using Local mode, StoryFox runs additional quality checks:
    - Validates character descriptions are complete
    - Parses character names, species, and visual traits
    - Enriches image prompts to guarantee character consistency
    
    This ensures your fox doesn't turn into a rabbit halfway through!
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Illustration generation">
    StoryFox generates images for:
    - Cover page (based on title and concept)
    - Every story page (based on the image prompt)
    
    Images generate concurrently in batches. You'll see the progress counter update as each illustration completes.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Handling errors

If generation fails:

- **Safety filter blocked**: The concept triggered content safety rules. Try rephrasing with gentler, child-friendly language.
- **Model unavailable**: For Local mode, ensure Apple Intelligence is enabled in System Settings. For Cloud mode, check your Hugging Face credentials in Settings.
- **Network error**: Check your internet connection (required for Cloud mode).

Click **Retry** to try again, or **Dismiss** to return to the creation screen.
