StoryFox includes a powerful App Intent that lets you queue story requests from Siri, Shortcuts, and other automation tools. This opens up creative workflows like generating stories from voice commands, combining multiple AI models, or triggering story creation from other apps.

## What is the "Create Story in StoryFox" intent?

The **Create Story in StoryFox** intent is an App Intent that queues a story concept in StoryFox with full control over book settings. When triggered, it:

1. Saves your story parameters to a queue
2. Opens StoryFox automatically
3. Populates the creation screen with your settings
4. Optionally starts generation immediately

This works seamlessly with Siri voice commands and the Shortcuts app, making it easy to create stories hands-free or as part of automated workflows.

## Adding the intent to Shortcuts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Shortcuts app">
    Launch **Shortcuts** from your Applications folder or Launchpad.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new shortcut">
    Click the **+** button in the toolbar to start building a new shortcut.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add the StoryFox action">
    - In the actions sidebar, search for "StoryFox" or "Create Story"
    - Drag **Create Story in StoryFox** into your shortcut
    
    You'll see a card with all available parameters.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure parameters">
    Fill in the story settings (see Parameters section below). You can:
    - Type static values directly
    - Use variables from previous actions
    - Leave parameters at their defaults
  </Step>

  <Step title="Name and save">
    Give your shortcut a descriptive name like "Quick Story" or "Bedtime Story Generator" and click **Done**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Available parameters

The intent accepts the following parameters:

<ParamField path="Story Concept" type="string" required>
  The main idea or theme for your story. This is the only required parameter.
  
  **Examples:**
  - "A brave mouse who explores a magical library"
  - "Two best friends who discover a hidden treehouse"
  - "A shy dragon learning to make friends"
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Pages" type="integer" default="8">
  Number of pages in your book. Must be an even number between 4 and 16.
  
  - **Minimum:** 4 pages
  - **Maximum:** 16 pages
  - **Default:** 8 pages
  
  <Tip>
    Shorter books (4-6 pages) generate faster and work well for simple concepts. Longer books (12-16 pages) allow more complex narratives.
  </Tip>
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Book Format" type="enum" default="standard">
  The page layout and aspect ratio for your book.
  
  - **Standard Square**: Classic square format, great for most stories
  - **Landscape**: Wide format, ideal for panoramic scenes
  - **Small Square**: Compact square format
  - **Portrait**: Tall format, good for vertical compositions
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Illustration Style" type="enum" default="illustration">
  Visual style for all images in the book.
  
  - **Illustration**: Polished, storybook-style artwork
  - **Animation**: Vibrant, animated look
  - **Sketch**: Hand-drawn, sketchy aesthetic
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Start Generation" type="boolean" default="true">
  Whether to begin generating the story immediately after opening StoryFox.
  
  - **true**: Story generation starts automatically (hands-free mode)
  - **false**: StoryFox opens with settings populated, but you must click **Squeeze a Story** manually
  
  <Tip>
    Set this to `false` if you want to review or tweak settings before generating.
  </Tip>
</ParamField>

## Step-by-step: Creating your first shortcut

Let's build a simple voice-activated shortcut that creates an 8-page illustrated story.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a new shortcut">
    Open Shortcuts and click **+** to create a new shortcut.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a text input action">
    Search for "Ask for Input" and add it to your shortcut.
    
    Configure it:
    - **Question:** "What should the story be about?"
    - **Input Type:** Text
    - **Default Answer:** (leave empty)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add the StoryFox intent">
    Search for "Create Story in StoryFox" and add it below the input action.
    
    Configure parameters:
    - **Story Concept:** Tap the field and select the "Provided Input" variable from the previous step
    - **Pages:** 8
    - **Book Format:** Standard Square
    - **Illustration Style:** Illustration
    - **Start Generation:** true
  </Step>

  <Step title="Name and test">
    Name your shortcut "Voice Story" and click **Done**.
    
    Run it by clicking the play button — Siri will ask what the story should be about, then queue it in StoryFox!
  </Step>
</Steps>

StoryFox registers the following voice triggers with Siri:

<CodeGroup>

```text Phrase 1
"Create a story with StoryFox"
```


</CodeGroup>

<Tip>
  You can also create custom Siri phrases by adding a "When I say..." automation in Shortcuts.
</Tip>

## How the app handles queued requests

When you trigger the App Intent, StoryFox uses a simple but robust queue system:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Intent saves request">
    The `QueueStoryInStoryFoxIntent` encodes your story parameters into a `ShortcutStoryRequest` and saves it to `UserDefaults` using the key `storyfox.shortcut.pendingRequest`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="StoryFox opens">
    The intent sets `openAppWhenRun: true`, so StoryFox launches immediately after queueing.
  </Step>

  <Step title="App consumes request">
    On launch, StoryFox's `consumePendingShortcutRequest()` method:
    - Checks for a pending request in `UserDefaults`
    - If found, removes it from the queue (consume-once pattern)
    - Navigates to the creation screen
    - Populates all form fields with your parameters
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generation starts (optional)">
    If you set `autoStart: true`, StoryFox immediately calls `viewModel.squeezeStory()` to begin generating the book.
    
    If `false`, the creation screen waits for you to click **Squeeze a Story**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Only one request is queued at a time.** If you trigger the intent again before opening StoryFox, the new request overwrites the previous one. This prevents queue buildup.
</Tip>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Intent doesn't appear in Shortcuts">
    Make sure you have StoryFox installed and have opened it at least once. App Intents register when the app first launches.
    
    If it still doesn't appear, try:
    1. Quitting and relaunching Shortcuts
    2. Restarting your Mac
    3. Reinstalling StoryFox
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="StoryFox opens but settings aren't populated">
    This usually means the request was consumed by a previous launch. The queue uses a consume-once pattern, so each request is only processed once.
    
    Run your shortcut again to queue a fresh request.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Page count is different than what I specified">
    Page counts are clamped to the valid range (4-16). If you pass an invalid number:
    - Values below 4 are set to 4
    - Values above 16 are set to 16
    - Odd numbers are rounded down to the nearest even number
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Siri phrases don't work">
    Make sure:
    1. Siri is enabled in **System Settings > Siri & Spotlight**
    2. StoryFox has been granted Shortcuts permissions
    3. You're using one of the registered phrases exactly as written
    
    You can also create custom Siri phrases through the Shortcuts app's automation tab.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
