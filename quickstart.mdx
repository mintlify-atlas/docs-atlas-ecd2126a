Get up and running with StoryFox by creating your first complete illustrated children's book from concept to finished product.

## Before you start

Ensure you have:

- **macOS 26** (Tahoe) running on Apple Silicon
- **Apple Intelligence** enabled on your device
- **StoryFox** installed from the [latest DMG](https://github.com/jakerains/StoryFox/releases/latest)

<Tip>
  For cloud generation features, you'll need a free Hugging Face account. You can configure this in Settings after your first story.
</Tip>

## Create your first story

<Steps>
  <Step title="Launch StoryFox">
    Open StoryFox from your Applications folder. You'll see the creation screen with a large text input field.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter a story idea">
    Type your story concept into the main text field. For your first story, try:
    
    ```
    A brave little robot who learns to make friends at robot school
    ```
    
    <Note>
      If you wait a few seconds, StoryFox will cycle through suggested story ideas using a typewriter animation. Click any suggestion to use it.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure your book">
    Click the **wand and stars** icon to open Book Setup and customize:
    
    - **Page count**: Choose 4-16 pages (default: 8)
    - **Format**: Standard Square, Landscape, Portrait, or Small Square
    - **Illustration style**: Illustration, Animation, or Sketch
    
    For your first book, the defaults work great.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start generation">
    Click the **Squeeze a Story** button. StoryFox will begin the generation process:
    
    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="Phase 1: Generating text" icon="text.alignleft">
        The AI creates your complete story including:
        - Title and author line
        - Character descriptions with visual details
        - Story text for each page (2-4 sentences)
        - Image prompts for every page
        
        Watch partial text stream in as the story is written (`CreationViewModel.swift:190`).
      </Accordion>
      
      <Accordion title="Phase 2: Validating characters" icon="checkmark.circle">
        StoryFox runs Foundation Model validation to ensure character descriptions are complete and consistent (`CreationViewModel.swift:215-226`).
        
        The system parses character names, species, colors, and traits to inject into every image prompt.
      </Accordion>
      
      <Accordion title="Phase 3: Generating images" icon="photo.on.rectangle">
        Illustrations generate concurrently for:
        - Cover page (based on title and concept)
        - All story pages (one per page)
        
        Progress updates show completed/total count as images finish (`CreationViewModel.swift:306-338`).
      </Accordion>
    </AccordionGroup>
    
    <Info>
      Generation takes 2-5 minutes depending on page count and whether you're using on-device or cloud models.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Read your book">
    When generation completes, your book opens automatically in the reader. Use:
    
    - **Arrow keys** or **swipe gestures** to navigate pages
    - **Full-screen button** to hide the sidebar
    - **Page grid** to jump to any page
  </Step>

  <Step title="Export and share">
    Click the **export** icon in the reader toolbar and choose:
    
    - **PDF** — 300 DPI print-ready file
    - **EPUB** — Fixed-layout EPUB 3.0 for digital readers
    
    Select a location and save your finished book.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding generation modes

StoryFox offers multiple text generation providers (`CreationViewModel.swift:98-112`):

| Provider | Type | When to use |
|----------|------|-------------|
| **Apple FoundationModels** | On-device | Default — works offline, no API key needed |
| **MLX Swift** | On-device | Run open-weight models locally (Qwen3, LFM2.5) |
| **Hugging Face Inference** | Cloud | Access larger models (GPT-OSS 120B, DeepSeek V3) |

<Tip>
  Start with Apple FoundationModels for your first story. It requires no setup and runs entirely on your Mac.
</Tip>

## What makes StoryFox different?

### Two-pass generation pipeline

Every story uses a two-pass process for better quality (`CreationViewModel.swift:188-245`):

1. **Pass 1** — AI writes complete story text with character descriptions
2. **Pass 2** — With full context, AI generates image prompts that maintain visual consistency across all pages

This means page 1 knows what happens on page 8 — characters look the same throughout.

### Character consistency

StoryFox parses character entries to extract (`CreationViewModel.swift:229-232`):
- Character names
- Species (dog, fox, robot, etc.)
- Visual traits (colors, clothing, accessories)
- Distinguishing features

These details are mechanically injected into every image prompt, ensuring your brave little robot looks the same on every page.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Safety filter blocked my concept">
    Apple's safety guardrails may block certain concepts. If you see a guardrail violation error (`CreationViewModel.swift:360-365`):
    
    - Rephrase with gentler, child-friendly language
    - Avoid words that might trigger content filters
    - Focus on positive themes and outcomes
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Model unavailable error">
    For on-device generation (`CreationViewModel.swift:114-136`):
    - Verify Apple Intelligence is enabled in System Settings
    - Ensure you're running macOS 26 on Apple Silicon
    
    For cloud generation:
    - Add Hugging Face credentials in Settings
    - Check your internet connection
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Generation is taking too long">
    - On-device generation is slower but private (2-5 minutes)
    - Cloud generation is faster (1-3 minutes)
    - More pages = longer generation time
    - Image generation runs concurrently to maximize speed
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
